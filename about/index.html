What I build

One job per tool, done fast

Most score-calculator sites bury a one-second estimate under ads, pop-ups, and a wall of text. I go the other way. Each calculator does one thing and loads right away.

Every AP tool carries the real exam structure, the question counts and the free-response weighting, because a 4 on AP Bio and a 4 on APUSH don't come from the same math. Cut-offs are estimated from publicly released College Board scoring worksheets, and the page tells you they're estimates, right next to your score.

I built the whole site on my own and I work on it full time. That's the only reason the tools stay quick and a feature request from a stranger gets read the same day.

The honest part

What's official, and what's an estimate

No score calculator can hand you an official number. College Board doesn't publish the exact annual cut scores, and the digital SAT is adaptive. So I don't pretend. The exam formats here are official. The cut-offs are clearly-labeled estimates, refreshed each cycle from released scoring worksheets and recent score distributions.

If a number looks off, or you want a subject added, or a tool would save you a step, send it over. Small fixes usually ship the same week. Cut-offs last reviewed June 2026. Not affiliated with the College Board, ACT, or ETS. AP® is a trademark of the College Board.
