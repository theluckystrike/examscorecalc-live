What changed on the enhanced ACT

On the enhanced ACT, the science and writing sections are optional and do not affect the Composite score. That single change breaks most of the ACT calculators still ranking today, because they average four sections. An old-format estimate and the one above can land on different whole numbers for the same test booklet, so check which rule a calculator used before you trust its number.

On the ACT, the Composite score is the average of the English, math, and reading scores, rounded to the nearest whole number. ACT scores are reported on a 1 to 36 scale. Science still earns its own scale score, and the tool prints it, marked as not part of the Composite, because colleges and scholarship readers can still see it.

Where these numbers come from

I transcribe the conversion tables from the official ACT National Online Practice Test 1 Scoring Key and Conversion Tables published by ACT, version-stamp them with the form they came from, and load them into the page as data. Your browser then does the lookups and the arithmetic in front of you. Nothing here is a model's memory of a curve.

The refusal is deliberate. When I built these estimators I made the tool print estimate unavailable for any exam or year whose vetted curve has not been loaded, because a plausible-looking raw-to-scale cutoff is the single easiest thing to get wrong in test prep, and a wrong one reads exactly like a right one.

Questions students ask

Which ACT form does this calculator use?
The tables come from the official ACT National Online Practice Test 1 Scoring Key and Conversion Tables from act.org, and the form name prints next to every estimate so you never have to guess which curve you got.
Does Science count toward my Composite?
On the enhanced ACT, the science and writing sections are optional and do not affect the Composite score. The tool scores Science anyway and shows it separately.
What if I leave a section blank?
English, Mathematics, and Reading all need a number correct that appears in the table, otherwise the Composite prints estimate unavailable. Science can stay empty with no effect on the Composite.
How close am I to the next whole number?
The result shows the unrounded three-section average beside the rounded Composite, so you can see whether you were a hair under the tipping point or comfortably over it.
When do official ACT scores come out?
ACT reports that over 97% of ACT scores are available online within 1 to 4 weeks after your test date. Every estimate here is UNOFFICIAL. Only the College Board or ACT can give you an official score.
